# Phase 3: Marketplace Launch (Weeks 3-5)

## Overview
Launching your first marketplace programs with optimized sales pages, strategic pricing, and promotional efforts. This phase focuses on getting your programs in front of potential buyers and generating initial sales.

## Timeline
**Weeks 3-5** - Launch and initial promotion

---

## 3.1 First Marketplace Listings

### Recommended Launch Sequence

Start with **2-3 programs** to test the market and learn the platform:

#### 1. 4-Week Speed Development Program
**Why Start Here**:
- **Quick win**: Lower commitment for buyers (4 weeks vs. 8-12 weeks)
- **Lower price point**: Easier to sell ($29-39)
- **Clear results**: Speed improvements are measurable
- **Your expertise**: Aligns perfectly with your speed training specialization

**Target Audience**:
- Athletes wanting to improve sprint speed
- Sport-specific athletes (basketball, football, soccer, track)
- Athletes preparing for combines/testing

**Key Selling Points**:
- "Increase Sprint Speed by 0.2 Seconds in 4 Weeks"
- "Athletic-feeling speed work, not just drills"
- "Based on proven methods used with NCAA athletes"

#### 2. 6-Week Power Development Program
**Why Second**:
- **Core expertise**: Power development is your specialty
- **Mid-length commitment**: Good balance of results and commitment
- **Higher value**: Justifies higher price point ($39-49)
- **Differentiates**: Power-focused programs are less common

**Target Audience**:
- Athletes wanting explosive power
- Jump athletes (basketball, volleyball, track jumpers)
- Athletes needing vertical/horizontal jump improvements

**Key Selling Points**:
- "Build Explosive Power in 6 Weeks"
- "Comprehensive power development (jumps, throws, Olympic lifts)"
- "Progressive system from foundation to peak power"

#### 3. 8-Week Complete Athletic Performance
**Why Third**:
- **Premium offering**: Higher price point ($59-79)
- **Comprehensive**: Appeals to serious athletes
- **Your full system**: Shows your complete programming approach
- **Higher value**: Justifies premium pricing

**Target Audience**:
- Serious athletes wanting complete development
- Off-season athletes with time to commit
- Athletes wanting periodized, professional programming

**Key Selling Points**:
- "Complete Athletic Development System"
- "20+ years of proven periodization methods"
- "From GPP to Sport-Specific Performance"

## 3.2 Sales Page Optimization

### Sales Page Structure

For each program, create a compelling sales page with these elements:

#### Headline (H1)
**Formula**: Clear benefit + specific outcome + time frame

**Examples**:
- "Increase Sprint Speed by 0.2 Seconds in 4 Weeks"
- "Build Explosive Power in 6 Weeks: Complete Athletic Performance System"
- "Transform Your Athletic Performance in 8 Weeks"

**Your Unique Angle**:
- Emphasize "athletic-feeling" programs
- Highlight speed/power specialization
- Mention track record (NCAA athletes, state qualifiers)

#### Tagline (Subheadline)
**Formula**: Your unique approach + target audience

**Examples**:
- "Sport-Specific Speed Training for Competitive Athletes"
- "Athletic-Feeling Power Development That Transfers to Sport"
- "Periodized Programming Built for Serious Athletes"

#### Description Structure

**1. Who It's For** (Target Audience):
- Specific sports/athletes
- Skill levels (beginner, intermediate, advanced)
- Training goals
- Equipment availability

**Example**:
> "This program is designed for competitive athletes who want to improve sprint speed. Whether you're a basketball player wanting faster first steps, a football player preparing for combines, or a track athlete looking to drop time, this program delivers measurable speed improvements."

**2. What They'll Achieve** (Specific Outcomes):
- Measurable results (speed times, jump distances, strength gains)
- Performance improvements
- Skill development

**Example**:
> "By the end of this 4-week program, you'll see:
> - Improved 10m and 30m sprint times
> - Better acceleration mechanics
> - Increased power output
> - Enhanced movement quality"

**3. What's Included** (Program Details):
- Training structure (days/week, session length)
- Exercise library access
- Coaching support
- Progress tracking

**Example**:
> "This program includes:
> - 4 weeks of progressive speed training (3-4 sessions/week)
> - Complete exercise library with video demonstrations
> - Detailed coaching cues and technique notes
> - Baseline and final performance testing protocols
> - 60-75 minute sessions designed for efficiency"

**4. Why You** (Your Expertise):
- Credentials and track record
- Unique programming approach
- Success stories

**Example**:
> "As a Certified High School Strength & Conditioning Coach with 10+ years of experience, I've developed speed programs for NCAA athletes and state qualifiers. This program uses the same periodized approach and athletic-feeling movements that have produced real results for competitive athletes."

#### Visuals

**High-Quality Images/Videos**:
- Exercise demonstrations (key movements)
- Training session previews
- Before/after performance data (if available)
- Program structure visuals

**Video Content**:
- Program overview video (2-3 minutes)
- Exercise demonstration highlights
- Training philosophy explanation

#### Sport Tags

**Tag Appropriately for Marketplace Searchability**:
- Primary sport tags (Basketball, Football, Track & Field, Soccer, etc.)
- Training focus tags (Speed, Power, Strength, Athletic Performance)
- Skill level tags (Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced)
- Equipment tags (Full Gym, Minimal Equipment, etc.)

## 3.3 Pricing Strategy

### Marketplace Fee Structure

**Your Attribution Links** (when you drive the sale):
- **Static Programs**: $1/month program length + 2.9% + $0.30
  - Example: 4-week program = $1 + 2.9% + $0.30
- **Team Subscriptions**: $1/month per subscription + 2.9% + $0.30

**Marketplace Match** (when TrainHeroic drives the sale):
- **Static Programs**: 30% commission + 2.9% + $0.30
- **Team Subscriptions**: You keep 70% (less credit card fees)

### Recommended Pricing

Based on your [[Pricing Model - Personal Training & Offseason Programming]] research:

#### 4-Week Speed Development
- **List Price**: $29-39
- **Your Net** (your attribution): ~$27-37
- **Your Net** (marketplace match): ~$20-27

**Rationale**: Lower commitment, entry-level price point, quick results

#### 6-Week Power Development
- **List Price**: $39-49
- **Your Net** (your attribution): ~$36-46
- **Your Net** (marketplace match): ~$27-34

**Rationale**: Mid-length, core expertise, higher value

#### 8-Week Complete Athletic Performance
- **List Price**: $59-79
- **Your Net** (your attribution): ~$55-75
- **Your Net** (marketplace match): ~$41-55

**Rationale**: Comprehensive, premium offering, full system

### Pricing Psychology

**Value Anchoring**:
- Show what similar coaching costs ($90-110/session × frequency)
- Compare to facility-based training ($400-600/month)
- Highlight included value (exercise library, video demos, support)

**Scarcity** (if applicable):
- "Limited spots" messaging (if offering support/community)
- "Early adopter" pricing (if launching with discount)

**Test & Adjust**:
- Start with mid-range pricing
- Monitor sales and adjust based on:
  - Conversion rates
  - Price objections
  - Market feedback
  - Competitive positioning

## 3.4 Launch Strategy

### Pre-Launch (Week 3)

**Preparation**:
- Finalize all program content
- Complete sales pages
- Create promotional materials
- Set up tracking (sales, conversions, feedback)

**Content Creation**:
- Social media posts previewing programs
- Exercise demonstration videos
- Program overview content
- Educational content (speed/power principles)

### Launch Week (Week 4)

**Day 1**: Soft Launch
- Publish programs to marketplace
- Share with personal network
- Post on social media
- Email existing contacts

**Days 2-3**: Content Push
- Exercise demonstration posts
- Program feature highlights
- Success story sharing (if available)
- Educational content

**Days 4-5**: Engagement
- Respond to questions/comments
- Share program updates
- Engage with potential buyers
- Collect feedback

### Post-Launch (Week 5)

**Optimization**:
- Review sales data
- Gather buyer feedback
- Refine sales pages based on questions
- Adjust pricing if needed

**Expansion**:
- Add more programs if initial ones perform well
- Create sport-specific variations
- Develop upsell opportunities

## 3.5 Promotion Channels

### Your Attribution Links

**Where to Share**:
- Social media (Instagram, Twitter, LinkedIn)
- Email list (if you have one)
- Personal network
- Coaching contacts
- Athlete referrals

**Why Attribution Links Matter**:
- You pay lower fees ($1/month + processing vs. 30% commission)
- You keep more revenue
- You control the sales process

### Content Marketing

**Educational Content**:
- "Speed Training Principles" blog posts/articles
- "Power Development for [Sport]" guides
- Exercise technique breakdowns
- Training philosophy explanations

**Social Media Strategy**:
- Exercise demonstrations
- Program previews
- Success stories/testimonials
- Educational tips
- Behind-the-scenes content

### Leverage Your Expertise

**Authority Building**:
- Share your programming philosophy
- Explain your periodization approach
- Highlight unique training methods (ISO work, contrast training)
- Show your exercise library depth

## Success Metrics

Track these metrics:
- **Sales Volume**: Number of programs sold
- **Conversion Rate**: Views to purchases
- **Average Program Price**: Revenue per sale
- **Attribution vs. Marketplace Match**: Which drives more sales
- **Customer Feedback**: Reviews, questions, testimonials
