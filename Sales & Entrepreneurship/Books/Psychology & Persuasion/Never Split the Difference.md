# Never Split the Difference

## Web Summary

"Never Split the Difference" translates high-stakes negotiation techniques from FBI hostage negotiation into practical tools for business and everyday life. Voss challenges traditional "win-win" or compromise-focused negotiation, emphasizing that negotiation is about emotional intelligence, not logical argument.

Key techniques include:

- **Tactical Empathy** - Acknowledging feelings without necessarily agreeing to understand the other party's perspective
- **Mirroring** - Repeating the last 1-3 words someone says to build rapport and elicit more information
- **Labeling** - Naming underlying emotions ("It sounds like..." or "It seems like...") to diffuse tension
- **Calibrated Questions** - Using "what" or "how" questions that start with these words to guide thinking while preserving the other party's sense of control
- **Embracing "No"** - Reframing "no" as a boundary setter that adds clarity, not as rejection

The book emphasizes:

- **"That's Right" Moments** - Summarizing the other party's perspective so they feel genuinely understood (more powerful than "you're right")
- **Anchoring** - Using odd numbers and strategic offers to shape expectations
- **The Ackerman Model** - Systematic approach to making offers and concessions
- **Voice and Tone** - Using a calm, slow "late-night DJ" voice to create safety and control
