# Phase 3: Post-Formation Legal and Tax Setup

## Receive Formation Documents
- [ ] Articles of Organization from Minnesota Secretary of State
- [ ] Confirmation from Inc Authority
- [ ] Save all documents in a secure folder

## Obtain EIN (Employer Identification Number)
- [ ] Inc Authority includes free EIN filing — complete their form
- [ ] Or apply directly at IRS.gov (Form SS-4, free, instant online)
- [ ] Save EIN confirmation letter

## Create Operating Agreement (Recommended)
- [ ] Draft single-member LLC Operating Agreement
- [ ] Include: business purpose, member contributions, profit/loss allocation, management structure
- [ ] Sign and date (keep with business records)
- [ ] *Note: Inc Authority offers this as paid add-on, or use template/attorney*

## Register with Minnesota Department of Revenue
- [ ] Visit www.revenue.state.mn.us
- [ ] Complete Application for Business Registration (Form ABR)
- [ ] Register for applicable tax types:
  - [ ] Sales and Use Tax (if selling digital products/programming)
  - [ ] Withholding Tax (only if you plan to hire employees)
  - [ ] Business income tax (LLC pass-through)
- [ ] *Note: Online coaching/programming may be exempt from sales tax — verify with MN Revenue*

## Set Up Business Banking
- [ ] Open business checking account (bring Articles of Organization, EIN, Operating Agreement)
- [ ] Get business debit card
- [ ] Set up online banking
- [ ] Order business checks (if needed)
- [ ] Consider business credit card (build business credit)

## Business Insurance
- [ ] General liability insurance ($350-$750/year for $1M coverage)
- [ ] Professional liability insurance (for coaching services)
- [ ] Verify coverage for: online coaching, diagnostic services, group classes, contractor work
- [ ] Get certificate of insurance (may be required by school district)
