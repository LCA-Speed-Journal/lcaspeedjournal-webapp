# Big Ideas

## Cheat Code Program
Training program with specific structure:

### Split: 6x p/wk
- **2x Upper**
- **2x Lower**
- **2x "Field-Days"**

### Block Structure
- **Block 1: Weeks 1-5**
  - Accel-Day: Contrast-Sprints
  - Squat-Day: Heel-Elevated SQ Focus (plus Contrast Work)
  - Upper-Push: BB Bench-Focused
  - MaxV-Day: 30-40yd Sprints
  - Hinge-Day: Olys, RDLs
  - Upper-Pull: Chin-Focused
- **Block 2: Weeks 6-10**
  - Progressive adjustments to Block 1 structure
  - Increased intensity and complexity

### Key Components
- Barefoot Warmup (as Daniel Bach)
- Speed Warmup: Prime-Times, 80% Effort Sprint, 90% Effort Sprint
- Power Warmup: Skips (For Height/Dist)
- Contrast Training: Resisted → Unresisted
- Strength/Power Work: Tuck Jumps, Hurdle-Hops, Repeat Broad-Jump
- Contrast Supersets: Heel Elevated Squat → Seated Box-Jump
- AMRAP Sets: For strength development
- Accessories and Prehab/Rehab work

# Web-Summary

Tim Riley is the host of the Coach Em Up podcast and creator of the Cheat Code training program, a 6-day split structure (2x Upper, 2x Lower, 2x Field-Days) that emphasizes pragmatic coaching principles. His approach balances skill development with strength development, avoiding over-complication while maintaining principle-based coaching. Riley's Cheat Code program includes sophisticated block periodization with progressive adjustments across 10-week cycles, integrating barefoot warmups, speed warmups with prime-times, power warmups, and contrast training (resisted → unresisted). His philosophy emphasizes practical, real-world coaching applications, recognizing that effective training doesn't require excessive complexity. Through his podcast and training programs, Riley has established himself as a leading voice in pragmatic strength and conditioning, helping coaches navigate the balance between scientific principles and practical implementation. His work with collaborators like Austin Jochum, Jake Tuura, and Joel Smith demonstrates his commitment to integrating diverse perspectives in coaching methodology.
