# Big Ideas

## 4x100m Relay Optimization
Ventura College 4x100m relay optimization.

### Relay Legs
- Legs 1, 3 will run on inside (left-half) and receive baton in right hand
- Legs 2, 4 will run on outside (right-half) and receive baton in left hand

### Relay Measurements
- **Old**: Acceleration Zone (10m) â†’ Exchange Zone (20m)
- **New**: No Accel-Zone, just 30m Exchange-Zone

### Typical Acceleration vs Deceleration
- 60% by 10m - 85% by 20m - 95% by 30m
- Decel: 98% at 80m - 95% at 90m-100m

### The Cardinal Rules of the Relay
- Outgoing runner sets up at very beginning of exchange zone
- Baton Exchange should happen near 20m mark to maximize outgoing-acceleration

### Determining When to "GO"
- First Piece - 16-24 steps from start of zone
- Second Piece - Measure Back 4 more Steps (creating "Go-Zone")
  - Outgoing leaves when incoming enters Go-Zone

### Guidance for Outgoing Runner
- May stand/crouch, but feet should be pointed down track
- Aggressive Start, don't come out with hesitation
- Extend soft, steady hand on verbal cue
- Grasp baton *after* placed in hand - don't go fishing
- Don't look back!

### Guidance for Incoming Runner
- Must push through zone - "Catching the Outgoing Runner"
- Give verbal cue at appropriate time
- Extend baton at appropriate time
- Maintain speed in zone *after* pass
- Stay in lane until all other teams have completed passes

### Methods of Exchanging Baton
- **Overhand Downsweep Pass** (American-Style Pass)
- **Push-Pass** - By far most popular with current US and International Teams
- **Underhand Upsweep Pass** - Used by Japan
