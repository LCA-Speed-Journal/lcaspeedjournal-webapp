# Big Ideas

## Simple Approach
EXOS simple approach covering big ideas and practical application.

### Big Ideas
- **Simple does not mean easy** - Clear and concise, get to point, but be excellent

### The 3 Buckets at EXOS
- **Position** - Root Skills, Fundamental Strength in various body-positions, etc.
- **Pattern** - Coordination and Body-Awareness
- **Power** - Neural Outputs

### How to Determine Who Belongs to What Bucket?
- **Vertical Jump Profile** is big assessment used at EXOS
  - Non CMJ, CMJ, Depth Jump (40cm Box)
  - Should see 5-15% increase from Non-CMJ to CMJ - EUR
  - Men should be 26", Women should be 19" - Nicole's Thresholds

### EXOS Session Outline
- **Pillar Prep** - Address individual needs (3-7mins)
- **Movement Prep** - Address needs of session (skills, etc.)
- **Movement Skills** - Efficiency and specific strength (Med-Ball work, etc.)
- **Strength and Power** - Supporting movements used in activity
- **Energy System Development** - Metabolic stress / conditioning
- **Regeneration** - Self-care

### Big Questions
- What are tools you actually use?
- What are tools you're really comfortable with leveraging?

### What do Athletes Need?
- Movement
- Mindset
- Nutrition
- Recovery
- Job of coach: how to blend pillars together and individualize to what each athlete needs

### On-Field Practical
- **Split-Drop into Acceleration** - Not immediate accel. Drop, switch (1-2x), then out
- **Wall-Drill** - Can be used to teach hip-projection
- **Heavy Sled-Walks** - Teaching forward lean and strength of extension
