- [[Speed Priority Program Design (Kula TFC '25)]]: [[Brian Kula]] TFC '25 presentation on speed priority program design covering energy towards high-proficiency in small areas, long-term culture and program design, key concepts (push > pull, loaded foot, negative foot-speed), structure themes (accel/decel vs maxV), and rough session outline.

- [[Sprint Club — Game Speed]]: Sprint Club content on game speed covering speed as power, track vs team sports differences, types of speed (MSS, RSA), developing maximal sprint speed (quality, frequency, programming), repeated sprint ability, and sample programming.
- [[Sprint Club — Peak Recovery]]: Sprint Club peak recovery content covering recovery pillars (sleep, energy, hydration, mental), recovery pyramid (mental → sleep → habits → mobility → other), sleep protocols (FFC vs CTC), natural sleep tricks, waking up faster, and recovery strategies for athletes.

- [[Sprint Club — Speed Bounce]]: Sprint Club speed bounce content covering plantar-flexor chain (calf muscles, Achilles, foot muscles), ankle stiffness (ability to absorb/produce force quickly), max eccentric foot-strength (exercises and protocols), foot reactive strength (reactive calf raises, KB raises), and speed bounce development methods.

- [[The Sprint Club — Horizontal Checklist]]: Sprint Club horizontal checklist covering objective (apply horizontal forces as fast as possible for as much distance as possible), KPIs (low heel recovery, the slice, ankle stiffness, increasing stride length, shin angle rises gradually), and point-based scoring system for assessment.
- [[The Sprint Club — Sprint Clinic]]: Sprint Club clinic content covering levels of speed (1-3 with benchmarks), the start (first 5m filming, benchmarks for stride rate, stride length, air-time, ground-time), acceleration (force-velocity profile, peak horizontal power, peak horizontal forces).
- [[The Sprint Club — Start Checklist]]: Sprint Club start checklist covering objective (launch fast, fly far), KPIs (hips projecting far, 45° posture or lower, mostly straight line from neck to toe, foot retraction, fast reaction), and point-based scoring system for block start assessment.
- [[The Sprint Club — Vertical Checklist]]: Sprint Club vertical checklist covering objective (apply vertical forces as fast as possible for necessary distance), KPIs (foot strike near COG, rear knee staying close to hips, landing with at least parallel knees, leg having minimal bend), and point-based scoring system for upright running mechanics.
- [[Weight Training to Improve Speed Acquisition]]: [[Boo Shexnayder]] (LSU) approach to weight training for speed covering understanding speed as neural quality, understanding strength (neuromuscular integration), training by body-type (smalls vs bigs), compatible training design, and managing intensity (high intensity supported by low density).
- [[What We Really Know about Speed & Power]]: [[Jim Kielbaso]] (IYCA) overview of speed and power science covering basing training in science (avoiding neuroticism vs making stuff up), speed definition (large forces quickly in right direction), key findings (orientation of force, force and mechanics), key principles (balancing overload and specificity), strength and speed relationship (2x bodyweight squat threshold), power specificity relative to velocity, and reactive agility considerations.
