# Learning About Speed with Carmelita Jeter
[[Carmelita Jeter]] – Missouri State University (Ex-Olympian)
## Sprint Mechanics
- Relaxed and Tall, with Finesse
- Finesse: floating across the top of the ground. Motion should be forward, not up and down. 
	- Efficient, Rhythmic Leg-Action — wheel-like
- Patience: need to exercise patience when executing mechanics — needs to be flow, not robotic
- Flexibility: critical to mastery, also insures an athlete against potential injury
	- Hip Mobility is huge
## The Warmup
- Warmup is an additional opportunity to work on mechanics and good posture
## The Role of the Coach
Job to assess athletes, see their potential, and expose them to the tools to maximize their abilities.
Also need to preserve their health — Get Them to the Starting Line **Healthy.**
- Programming needs to meet the individual needs of the athlete. 
	- Addressing limiting factors and combine in a manner in which the athlete can round out their weaknesses while leveraging their strengths
- Leverage Intuitive-Coaching
## Coach Jet’s Ideal Schedule
In Sum: need a 24hr “nothing” before competition, and can’t stack too much work in a row
- M, T — Practices
- W — Off
- Th, F — Practices
- Sa — Warmup
- Su — Off
