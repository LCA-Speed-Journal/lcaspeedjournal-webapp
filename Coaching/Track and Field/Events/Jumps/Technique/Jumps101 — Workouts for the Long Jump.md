# Jumps101 — Workouts for the Long Jump
## Programming
## The Warmup
(Stolen from [[Dan Pfaff]] with some modifications)
- A-Skip — Coach Briggs cues the landing just barely staggered
- B-Skip – same idea, landing flat-footed
- Backwards Skipping
- Backwards Walking
- Lateral Walking — keeping the toes facing forwards
- Crossovers — 
- High-Knees — Heel under butt
- Dribble-Series — Ankle, Calf, Knee
- Long Prime-Times
- Bent-Knee Prime-Times
- Single-Leg Recoveries — (we know as the Quick-Leg Drill)
- Leg-Swings
- Hurdle-Recoveries (as trail-leg mechanics, just with rhythm)
- Strides — Moderate-Effort accels with good mechanics
## Training Cycle 1 — Base-Training
### Day 1
4-6 Rounds of 10-Rep Circuits — SQ/SQ-Jump, MB-Toss/Burpee, Pushups, Cherry-Pickers
### Day 2
Tempo Running — 4-6 x 250m @ 70-80%
Backwards Walking 20yds (sleds or hills)
### Day 3
3 x AMRAP — Tib-Raises, Calf-Raises, Core (2 Exercises for a 5min clock)

### Day 3
(As Cycle 1)
## Training Cycle 3 — Competition Training
### Day 1 — Jumps Practice
- 4 x Runway-Runs from 100ft
- 6 x 4-Step Gallop to Pop
- 6 x 6-10-Step long-jump pop-ups — hips high, travel into the pit, run through landing
- 4-6 x 6-10-Step long-jump — work on all mechanics
### Day 2 — Speed Work
6-8 x 30-60m with a 10m Build-in
### Day 3 — Plyos
- Bounds — 4-6 x 30-50m
- Gallops — 30m
- Power Skips — 30m
- Hurdle Hops — 4-6 jumps over 12-30”
